Lebanon

لبنان بلد ذات طبيعة ساحرة وتاريخ غني، ويعتبر وجهة سياحية شهيرة في الشرق الأوسط. تتميز السياحة في لبنان بتنوعها الكبير، حيث يمكن للزوار الاستمتاع بالشواطئ الرملية والمناظر الجميلة للبحر الأبيض المتوسط ، وزيارة الجبال الجميلة في الأطلس والقادسية. كما يضم لبنان العديد من المواقع الأثرية والتاريخية، مثل مدينة بيروت القديمة ، ومعبد باعلبك الروماني ، وقلعة صيدا البحرية. تتوفر العديد من النشاطات الرياضية والترفيهية في لبنان، بما في ذلك التزلج في منتجعات الأرز الشهيرة ، والمشي في الطبيعة الخلابة، والتسوق في الأسواق التقليدية والمراكز التجارية الحديثة. بالإضافة إلى ذلك، تتمتع لبنان بثقافة متنوعة ومطبخ لذيذ يجذب الزوار من جميع أنحاء العالم.

Lebanon is a country with a charming nature, rich maps, and a famous tourist destination in the Middle East. Tourism in Lebanon is characterized by its diversity, as you can enjoy beautiful beaches, beautiful views of the Mediterranean Sea, and a beautiful visit to the Atlas and Qadisiyah. It also includes many of Lebanon's archaeological and historical sites, such as the Old City of Beirut, the Roman Temple of Baalbek, and the Sidon Sea Castle. Lebanon features many modern sports activities, including sports activities at the famous Rice Resorts, nature walks, and shopping at entertainment centers. In addition, enjoying a diverse Lebanese culture that is attractive to visitors from all over the world.

Bsharri
Bsharri is located on the border of Wadi Qadisha in the North Governorate in Lebanon. The word Bsharri originally goes back to the Phoenician language, which means “Ishtar’s house.” She is one of the gods that the Phoenicians worshiped in the past. This town is the birthplace of the famous poet, painter, and sculptor Khalil Gibran, who now has a museum in the city to honor him. The nearby site of the sacred Qadisha Valley also houses some of the oldest Christian monastic communities in the Middle East; It is considered a true heritage of Maronite Christianity. Maronite Christians fleeing persecution took refuge in its mountainous terrain in the seventh century AD, and the Kadisha Valley subsequently became the spiritual center of the Maronite Church. The people of Bsharri are brave and tribal and are particularly known for their distinctive dialect when they speak Lebanese Arabic.

Batroun
“Batroun” is considered one of the oldest historical and tourist cities in the world, and its population dates back about five thousand years. It is a coastal city located in northern Lebanon, about 50 kilometers from the capital, Beirut, and about 30 kilometers west of the Tripoli city.The city of Batroun carries out entertainment and artistic activities in the summer. In 2009, the “Batroun International Festival” was held for the first time, and began hosting local and international artists in July and August of each year, in the old port area in Batroun.

Byblos
While historians say that fishermen were the ones who built the city near the coast, the legend from which the city's name was derived says that the god El - the chief god of the Canaanites - was the one who founded it, where springs were abundant, so the name Byblos was an abbreviation of "Geb El", meaning the spring of the god El. The history of the city of Byblos dates back more than eight thousand years, and historians believe that the Lebanese city of Byblos is the first city in the world that has been inhabited for eight thousand years.

Jounieh
The Jounieh cable car, or the Lebanon cable car for foreign tourists, is one of the most famous landmarks of Mount Lebanon. Its height at its highest point is 550 meters, while its length is 1,570 meters. It connects the Bay of Jounieh and the Harissa area. It is considered one of the most beautiful tourist places in Mount Lebanon. The idea of ​​establishing the Jounieh cable car began by some young men who They were taking a school trip to the Shrine of Our Lady of Lebanon and dreamed of creating a means to transport visitors to this region directly from Jounieh to Harissa. After that, the process of constructing the cable car began by the government and it was officially opened in 1965 to become one of the most important tourist destinations in the country. It is 16 km north of Beirut.

Tyre
The city of Tyre is one of the oldest inhabited cities in the world. It was one of the oldest ancient Phoenician capitals, and it is called the Lady of the Seas. This historic city overlooks the Mediterranean Sea, and is filled with great Roman monuments. Many tourists coming from different regions visit it because it is full of tourist attractions, as tourists will find many facilities that make their visit to this city an unforgettable trip. It has picturesque beaches, historical and artistic museums, and archaeological sites such as the Roman horse racing field, in addition to modern shopping centers that combine fun, entertainment, fashion, delicious food, and distinctive hotels and tourist resorts.

Baalbak
The Lebanese city of Baalbek, the city of the sun, one of the most important tourist cities in Lebanon, a heritage city, as heritage should be; Its roots go deep into history. Tourism in Baalbek: Its strategic location as a transit area and a crossroads for caravans in the past, between northern Syria and northern Palestine on the one hand, and between the Levantine mainland and the Mediterranean coast on the other hand. It made it stable for many successive civilizations, which left behind unique archaeological and historical monuments. It also increases the beauty of the city of Baalbek. It is located in the middle of the Bekaa Valley with its vast green areas, interspersed with the Litani River.

Bekaa
The Bekaa, one of Lebanon's governorates, is a wide plain extending between the eastern and western Lebanese mountain ranges. Its lands are fertile and the two largest rivers in Lebanon flow through it: the Litani and the Orontes. Therefore, during the previous centuries, this region formed the Levant's reservoir of grains, vegetables, and fruits. The Bekaa Valley constitutes an obligatory corridor between northern Syria and Palestine, as it was crossed by caravans at times and invaders at other times. Today, it is considered the largest agricultural area in Lebanon, dotted with farms, villages, fields, and vineyards that cover the plain with a multi-colored scarf. Your path is often blocked by agricultural tractors that harvest sugar beets, and shepherds who lead herds of sheep and goats on both sides of the path. It is best to always carry in your hand. A camera to capture the amazing views that surprise you around the corners.

Zahle
The city of Zahle is one of the largest and most populous Lebanese cities, and it also enjoys a distinct strategic geographical location. It is bordered on the northern side by Mount Sannine and on the southern side by the Bekaa Valley. The Berdouni River passes through it, which divides it into approximately two equal halves. The city also enjoys... With a moderate climate throughout the year, green areas and fertile lands, which makes agriculture one of the most common activities carried out by its residents. The city has a long history, as it witnessed and lived with many peoples and tribes who left archaeological monuments in it that still exist to this day, such as the Jamal Pasha Hospital, which was built in 1914 AD, and old markets such as the Balat Market, which was built in 1888 AD.
